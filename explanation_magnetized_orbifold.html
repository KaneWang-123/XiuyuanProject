Magnetized Orbifold Models

H. Abe, T. Kobayashi, H. Ohki — arXiv:0806.4748v2 (YITP-08-48 / KUNS-2145)

1. What Is the Paper About?

The paper studies how to build realistic four-dimensional particle physics models from higher-dimensional string-inspired gauge theory by combining two ingredients:

  • Magnetic fluxes in the compact extra dimensions — these produce chiral fermions and determine the number of particle generations.
  • Orbifold projections — these impose discrete symmetry identifications on the extra-dimensional space, further projecting the spectrum.

The central discovery is that combining both ingredients gives a new class of flavor structures — in particular, the orbifold projection can drastically reduce the generation count compared to the magnetized torus alone, and it allows a new type of zero-mode wave function (odd-parity modes) that does not appear without magnetic fluxes. This opens new model-building possibilities not available in either pure orbifold or pure magnetized torus models.

2. Setup: Super Yang-Mills on Extra Dimensions

Start with $\mathcal{N}=1$ super Yang-Mills theory in $D = 4+2n$ dimensions. The Lagrangian is:

$$\mathcal{L} = -\frac{1}{4g^2}\,\mathrm{Tr}\!\left(F^{MN}F_{MN}\right) +\frac{i}{2g^2}\,\mathrm{Tr}\!\left(\bar\lambda\,\Gamma^M D_M\lambda\right),$$

where $\lambda$ are the gaugino fields in $D$ dimensions and $M,N = 0,\ldots,D-1$. The extra dimensions are taken to be the factorizable torus $(T^2)^n$ or its orbifold. Fields are Kaluza-Klein decomposed as

$$\lambda(x,y) = \sum_n \chi_n(x)\otimes \psi_n(y),$$

where $\chi_n(x)$ is the four-dimensional spinor and $\psi_n(y)$ the internal wavefunction. The four-dimensional massless spectrum is determined by the zero-modes $\psi_n(y)$ satisfying the Dirac equation in the compact space.

3. Magnetized Torus: Review

3.1 $U(1)$ on $T^2$ — Landau levels and chiral zero-modes

On $T^2$ with coordinates $(y_4, y_5)$, introduce a constant magnetic flux $F_{45} = 2\pi M$ ($M \in \mathbb{Z}$). The gauge is chosen as $A_4 = 0$, $A_5 = 2\pi M y_4$. The zero-mode Dirac equation for a spinor of charge $q$ is:

$$\tilde\Gamma^m(\partial_m - iq A_m)\,\psi(y) = 0, \qquad m = 4,5.$$

The boundary conditions around the two cycles of $T^2$ are:

$$\psi(y_4+1, y_5) = e^{2\pi i q M y_5}\,\psi(y_4,y_5), \qquad \psi(y_4,y_5+1) = \psi(y_4,y_5).$$

Consistency requires $M \in \mathbb{Z}$. For $qM > 0$, there are exactly $|M|$ normalizable zero-modes for $\psi_+$, with wave functions given by Jacobi theta-functions:

$$\Theta^j(y_4,y_5) = N_j\,e^{-M\pi y_4^2}\, \vartheta\!\left[\begin{array}{c} j/M \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\!\left(M(y_4+\tau y_5),\,M\tau\right), \qquad j = 0,1,\ldots,M-1,$$

where $\tau$ is the complex structure modulus of $T^2$. These are Gaussian-like profiles quasi-localized at different positions in the extra dimension. The opposite chirality $\psi_-$ has no normalizable zero-modes, giving a chiral 4D theory.

Key fact on the torus: Magnetic flux $M$ on $T^2$ gives exactly $|M|$ chiral zero-modes. On the full $(T^2)^3$, the bi-fundamental matter between stacks $a$ and $b$ has $$I_{ab} = \prod_{i=1}^3 (M_a^{(i)} - M_b^{(i)})$$ independent zero-modes. To get 3 generations one needs $I_{ab} = 3$.

3.2 Yukawa couplings from overlap integrals

Four-dimensional Yukawa couplings are computed as the overlap integral of three zero-mode wave functions in the extra dimensions:

$$Y^{ijk} = g \int dy\;\Theta^{i_1,i_2,i_3}(y)\cdot\Theta^{j_1,j_2,j_3}(y)\cdot\Theta^{k_1,k_2,k_3}(y),$$

where $\Theta^{i_1,i_2,i_3}(y) = \Theta^{i_1}(y_4,y_5)\,\Theta^{i_2}(y_6,y_7)\,\Theta^{i_3}(y_8,y_9)$. Hierarchical Yukawa couplings arise naturally when different generations are quasi-localized far from each other in the extra dimensions.

3.3 The problem: too many families on the torus

On $(T^2)^3$ with the supersymmetry condition

$$\sum_{i=1}^3 \pm\frac{M_a^{(i)}-M_b^{(i)}}{\mathcal{A}^{(i)}} = 0$$

(where $\mathcal{A}^{(i)}$ is the area of the $i$-th torus), it turns out that achieving exactly three families is very constrained. Generic supersymmetric flux choices give many more families (e.g., 6, 12, ...) and very large numbers of Higgs fields. This motivates the orbifold construction.

4. Key New Ingredient: The Orbifold $T^2/\mathbb{Z}_2$

4.1 $\mathbb{Z}_2$ identification and parity projection

The orbifold $T^2/\mathbb{Z}_2$ identifies $(y_4,y_5) \sim (-y_4,-y_5)$. The magnetic flux $F_{45} = 2\pi M$ is invariant under this identification. Spinor fields must satisfy the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ boundary condition:

$$\psi(-y_4,-y_5) = (-i)\tilde\Gamma^4\tilde\Gamma^5\,P\,\psi(y_4,y_5),$$

where $P = \pm 1$ (depending on the field's charge and an integer $n$). This projects the $M$ torus zero-modes onto their even or odd parts:

$$\Theta^j_{\rm even} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\Theta^j + \Theta^{M-j}\right), \qquad \Theta^j_{\rm odd} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\Theta^j - \Theta^{M-j}\right).$$

This uses the reflection property $\Theta^j(-y_4,-y_5) = \Theta^{M-j}(y_4,y_5)$. The number of surviving zero-modes depends on $M$ and the parity $P$:

Novel feature: On the pure orbifold ($M=0$), odd-parity modes are always massive — they cannot be zero-modes. But with magnetic flux $M \neq 0$, the $\Theta^j$ and $\Theta^{M-j}$ mix together, and their odd linear combinations can be normalizable zero-modes. This is a qualitatively new phenomenon that does not appear in either the magnetized torus or the pure orbifold alone.

5. Yukawa Selection Rules from the Orbifold

The 3-point Yukawa couplings on the orbifold are overlap integrals of the projected wave functions. Because odd functions vanish at the orbifold fixed points $y=0$, there are new selection rules. Specifically:

$$\int dy\;\Theta^i_{\rm even}\cdot\Theta^j_{\rm even}\cdot\Theta^k_{\rm odd} = 0, \qquad \int dy\;\Theta^i_{\rm odd}\cdot\Theta^j_{\rm odd}\cdot\Theta^k_{\rm odd} = 0,$$

while the following are generically non-vanishing:

$$\int dy\;\Theta^i_{\rm even}\cdot\Theta^j_{\rm odd}\cdot\Theta^k_{\rm odd} \neq 0, \qquad \int dy\;\Theta^i_{\rm even}\cdot\Theta^j_{\rm even}\cdot\Theta^k_{\rm even} \neq 0.$$

These selection rules enforce a non-trivial texture on the Yukawa matrix — some entries are exactly zero by the orbifold parity, while others are allowed. This is a key difference from the magnetized torus, where all $I_{ab}^3$ entries can in principle be nonzero.

Additionally, brane modes (fields localized at orbifold fixed points) can only couple to bulk zero-modes with even wave functions, since odd wave functions vanish at the fixed points:

$$\Theta^j_{\rm odd}(y=0) = 0.$$

This decoupling is another new feature of magnetized orbifold models.

6. Six-Dimensional Orbifolds and Three-Family Models

The full construction uses the six-dimensional orbifolds $T^6/\mathbb{Z}_2$ or $T^6/(\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}'_2)$. The two $\mathbb{Z}_2$ actions act on coordinates as:

$$\mathbb{Z}_2:\quad y_m \to -y_m~(m=4,5,6,7),\quad y_n \to y_n~(n=8,9),$$ $$\mathbb{Z}'_2:\quad y_m \to -y_m~(m=4,5,8,9),\quad y_n \to y_n~(n=6,7).$$

After both projections, the surviving bi-fundamental zero-modes on $T^6/(\mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}'_2)$ have wave functions of the form:

$$\Theta^{j_1}_{\rm even}(y_4,y_5)\,\Theta^{j_2}_{\rm even}(y_6,y_7)\,\Theta^{j_3}_{\rm even}(y_8,y_9) \quad\text{or}\quad \Theta^{j_1}_{\rm odd}(y_4,y_5)\,\Theta^{j_2}_{\rm odd}(y_6,y_7)\,\Theta^{j_3}_{\rm odd}(y_8,y_9).$$

6.1 Explicit three-family model (Model 1)

Starting from 10D $U(N)$ SYM on $\mathbb{R}^{3,1}\times T^6/(\mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}'_2)$, take $N_a=4, N_b=2, N_c=2$ (Pati-Salam model) with magnetic fluxes:

$$F_{45} = 2\pi\,\mathrm{diag}(0\cdot\mathbf{1}_{N_a},\,-3\cdot\mathbf{1}_{N_b},\,-4\cdot\mathbf{1}_{N_c}),$$ $$F_{67} = 2\pi\,\mathrm{diag}(0\cdot\mathbf{1}_{N_a},\,-4\cdot\mathbf{1}_{N_b},\,-1\cdot\mathbf{1}_{N_c}),$$ $$F_{89} = 2\pi\,\mathrm{diag}(0\cdot\mathbf{1}_{N_a},\,-1\cdot\mathbf{1}_{N_b},\,+3\cdot\mathbf{1}_{N_c}).$$

This satisfies the SUSY condition $\sum_i \pm(M_a^{(i)}-M_b^{(i)})=0$. The flux difference for the $ab$ sector is $(I^1_{ab}, I^2_{ab}, I^3_{ab}) = (3, 4, 1)$, which on the torus alone would give $|I_{ab}|=12$ families. After the $\mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}'_2$ orbifold projection with

$$P = P' = \mathrm{diag}(\mathbf{1}_{N_a},\,-\mathbf{1}_{N_b},\,\mathbf{1}_{N_c}),$$

only $\Theta^{j_1}_{\rm odd}\,\Theta^{j_2}_{\rm even}\,\Theta^{j_3}_{\rm even}$ survive (since $I^3_{ab}=1$ has no odd mode). The family count becomes $I^1_{ab(\rm odd)}\times I^2_{ab(\rm even)}\times I^3_{ab(\rm even)} = 1\times 3\times 1 = 3$.

6.2 Explicit three-family model (Model 2)

Using different magnetic fluxes:

$$F_{45} = 2\pi\,\mathrm{diag}(0,\,-3,\,+1),\quad F_{67} = 2\pi\,\mathrm{diag}(0,\,-4,\,-5),\quad F_{89} = 2\pi\,\mathrm{diag}(0,\,-1,\,-4),$$

and different projections $P = \mathrm{diag}(-\mathbf{1}_{N_a},\,\mathbf{1}_{N_b},\,\mathbf{1}_{N_c})$, $P' = \mathrm{diag}(\mathbf{1}_{N_a},\,-\mathbf{1}_{N_b},\,\mathbf{1}_{N_c})$:

In Model 2, Yukawa couplings are allowed. Moreover, because the three generations are quasi-localized at different positions on the second $T^2$, but one generation of $\lambda^{ab}$ and $\lambda^{ca}$ is co-localized with the Higgs $\lambda^{cb}$, the model naturally gives one large and two small Yukawa couplings — a qualitative explanation of the top-bottom hierarchy.

7. Summary of the Main Results

  1. Generation number from magnetic flux: On the torus, the number of chiral zero-modes for bi-fundamental matter is $I_{ab} = \prod_i(M_a^{(i)}-M_b^{(i)})$.
  2. Orbifold reduces generation number: The $\mathbb{Z}_2$ projection keeps only even or odd combinations of the $|I^i_{ab}|$ torus modes on each $T^2$. On $T^6/(\mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}'_2)$, the surviving modes are products of all-even or all-odd combinations. This can drastically reduce the total count, e.g., from 12 to 3 families.
  3. New type of zero-mode (odd modes): In the presence of magnetic flux, odd-parity functions become normalizable zero-modes. This is absent in pure orbifold models and qualitatively changes the flavor structure.
  4. New Yukawa selection rules: The orbifold parity enforces $\int \Theta_{\rm even}\,\Theta_{\rm even}\,\Theta_{\rm odd} = 0$ and $\int \Theta_{\rm odd}\,\Theta_{\rm odd}\,\Theta_{\rm odd} = 0$, creating texture zeroes in the Yukawa matrix not present on the torus.
  5. Decoupling of brane modes: Fields localized at orbifold fixed points cannot couple to bulk zero-modes with odd wave functions, since $\Theta^j_{\rm odd}(0) = 0$. This is entirely new compared to standard orbifold models.
  6. Semi-realistic models: Two explicit Pati-Salam three-family models are constructed. Model 2 gives a qualitative explanation of Yukawa hierarchy from geometric localization in the extra dimensions.
